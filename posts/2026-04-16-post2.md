# Why Structured Feedback is Necessary in Goal-Oriented Community Interactions

## Problem Context

In many online communities, users are able to comment freely on shared content without any constraints. While this enables open participation, it often leads to feedback that is loosely related to the poster’s intention and difficult to apply in practice.

This is particularly problematic in situations where users are seeking feedback on a specific aspect of their work rather than general opinion.

## Problem Analysis

Free-form feedback lacks guidance, meaning that reviewers rely on their own assumptions about what is important. As a result, responses often reflect personal preferences rather than the poster’s intended focus.

For example, a user seeking feedback on clarity or usability may instead receive comments about visual style or personal taste. While these responses are not necessarily incorrect, they are not aligned with the original goal.

This misalignment creates two key issues: first, it makes it difficult for users to identify which feedback is actionable; second, it can reduce engagement, as users may feel that the feedback they receive is not useful.

## Design Decision

To address this issue, this project adopts a structured feedback approach. Instead of allowing unrestricted comments, users are guided to provide feedback through predefined prompts such as:

- What is clear?
- What is confusing?
- What could be improved?

These prompts are designed to anchor responses around specific aspects of the user experience, reflecting principles from design critique and usability testing, where feedback is most effective when grounded in a defined goal.

In addition, feedback is explicitly aligned with a stated feedback goal provided by the original poster. This ensures that responses remain relevant and interpretable within the intended context.

## Alternative Approaches

One alternative would be to retain free-form feedback while providing optional prompts. This would preserve flexibility, but it does not ensure that users follow the intended structure, and feedback may remain inconsistent.

Another alternative would be fully automated or AI-generated feedback. While this may provide consistency, it introduces technical complexity and reduces the role of human judgement within the community.

Therefore, structured peer feedback represents a balance between flexibility and guidance.

## Trade-offs

Structured feedback improves the relevance and clarity of responses, but reduces flexibility in how users express their thoughts.

It also introduces additional effort, as users must interpret prompts and respond within a defined structure. This may reduce participation in some cases.

However, these trade-offs are acceptable, as the system prioritises meaningful, goal-oriented interaction over open-ended social commentary.

## Conclusion

Structured feedback is a necessary mechanism in community systems that aim to support goal-oriented interaction. By guiding users to respond within a shared context, it improves the relevance, clarity, and usefulness of feedback, enabling more effective interpretation and iteration.
